I met Vaughan Jones at the IHES (Bures sur Yvette) when he was working on the Jones polynomials. He had made a list of braids which represented all prime knots up to ten crossings, for testing. He gave me this list, initially for a private basic program, but later allowed us to use it publicly in our program 3D-XplorMath. Here the same old list is used. Jones commented that this early list may contain errors and also that he did not always find the shortest braid word. It seems he did not make his list public, but I view it as a piece of mathematical history.
The braid words Nr 166 and Nr 201 cannot be correct, because '1111-2-21-212' and '-12-32112-32-3' can be represented with less than 10 crossings; moreover Nr 201 is a 2-component link. The following sign change: '1111-2-21-21-2' gives an alternating knot with 10 crossings. I cannot check whether this attempted correction gives the intended knot Nr 166. Nr 201 is the only braid with more than 1 component in Jones' list.
